How investors find startups on Dealflow Scout

Investors use Scout AI to search, screen, and score companies daily. Here's how your startup gets discovered.

🔍

Thesis-matched discovery

Investors configure their investment thesis — sector, stage, geography, team signals — and Dealflow Scout surfaces the companies that match. If your startup fits what an investor is looking for, you appear in their daily feed, automatically.

📡

Signal-driven momentum tracking

Our platform tracks 7 live data sources daily — including GitHub activity, job postings, product launches, and funding news. Companies showing strong momentum signals get surfaced to investors even before they're well-known.

🤖

Scout AI proactive outreach

When an investor asks Scout AI to draft outreach, it uses your public signals to craft a personalized message. The best founders in our database receive 3–5x more investor interest than those with thin or outdated profiles.

What signals matter to investors

Dealflow Scout's AI scoring model evaluates startups across 94 signals. Here are the ones that move the needle most.

📈
Headcount growth velocity
Companies growing engineering headcount by 50%+ quarter-over-quarter score highest. Hiring signals are the single strongest predictor of momentum in our model.
⚡
Product launch activity
New product releases on Product Hunt, GitHub commits, and changelog activity signal an active, shipping team — a top 3 signal for early-stage investors.
🎓
Founding team pedigree
Prior startup exits, engineering backgrounds from top companies, and repeat founder status all significantly boost your thesis match score with tier-1 funds.
💰
Funding stage & raise history
We track capital raised, investor names, and time between rounds. Efficient capital deployment and strong co-investors are strong credibility signals.
📣
Press & media mentions
Coverage in TechCrunch, Bloomberg, and niche trade media generates real-time alerts for investors tracking your category. We scan GDELT and 200+ outlets daily.
